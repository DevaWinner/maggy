What visual design elements did you consider as you were preparing your photograph?

In the recreated photo, I clearly paid attention to the several design elements below:

  • Movement (Line + Form): I captured the sweeping motion of the braids, creating long dynamic lines similar to the original. This movement became the dominant feature of the image.
  • Background + Environment (Space): I chose a beach setting just like the original. The open space and horizon helped the action in the foreground stand out.
  • Contrast: Even though my photo was taken during the day, the dark outfit against the softer sky created a good contrast, highlighting my muse silhouette.

What specific things did you do to imitate the original?

I recreated the exact dramatic head-flip movement that defines the original, which captures the same sense of energy and flow. I shot the photo at the beach to imitate the natural, open-water background used in the original image. I also used a wide open space around the subject, keeping the focus on the muse and the hair, just like it was in the original.

What is one key thing you learned from this picture that you could use to improve your work?

The key lesson I learnt from recreating this image is the power of timing and movement in photography.
